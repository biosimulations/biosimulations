Sharing and reusing biomodels, simulations, and visualizations
BioSimulations is a free platform for sharing and re-using biomodels, simulations, and visualizations. BioSimulations makes sharing and reuse easy by providing a simple web interface for a wide range of frameworks (e.g., constraint-based, continuous kinetic, discrete kinetic), algorithms (e.g., FBA, SSA), formats (e.g., SBML), and tools (e.g., COBRApy, COPASI, libRoadRunner).
Helping authors, reviewers, and investigators share and reuse biomodeling studies
Users
Reuse published modeling studies
BioSimulations provides investigators a central portal for finding models, simulations, and visualizations; modifying simulations and visualizations; executing simulations; and painting visualizations with simulation results.
Authors
Publish modeling studies
BioSimulations makes it easy for authors to share models, simulations, and visualizations. Sharing modeling studies through BioSimulations makes it easier for other inevstigators to find and reuse them.
Reviewers
Evaluate modeling studies
    BioSimulations makes it easy for reviewers to evaluate models, simulations, and visualizations. Reviewers can interactively explore studies, verify the reproducibility of reported results, and test models under alternate conditions.

About BioSimulations

BioSimulations was developed by the Center for Reproducible Biomedical Modeling , the Karr Lab at the Icahn School of Medicine at Mount Sinai , and the Moraru Lab at the University of Connecticut Health Center with support from the National Institutes of Health and the National Science Foundation.

BioSimulations is implemented using several open-source tools and cloud platforms. The models, simulations, and visualizations are available under the licenses specified for each resource. The code is openly available under the MIT license .
