Models are powerful tools for understanding and predicting the behavior of complex systems. More comprehensive and more predictive models could help biologists understand the physical basis of behavior, help physicians understand disease, and help bioengineers design biology. One promising way to build more comprehensive models is to combine multiple models of individual subsystems. Toward more comprehensive models, BioSimulations makes it easy for researchers to share and reuse models.
BioSimulations accepts all modeling frameworks. Currently, BioSimulations can execute the Flux Balance (e.g., FBA), non-spatial continuous kinetic (e.g., ODE), and non-spatial discrete kinetic (e.g., SSA) frameworks.
BioSimulations accepts all model formats. However, we recommend using standards such as the Systems Biology Markup Language (SBML) . Currently, BioSimulations can execute the BioNetGen Language (BNGL) and SBML.
BioSimulations accepts models that require any simulation tool. Currently, BioSimulations can execute models using CBMPy , COBRApy , COPASI , iBioSim , GillesPy2 , NFSim , PySCeS , tellurium/libRoadRunner , and VCell .
Authors can provide an image, description, tags, taxon, parent models, and references. For each variable and parameter, authors can provide a description, ontology terms, value, recommended range, and units.
Authors can keep their models private or share them with the world. Authors can help others reuse their models by also publishing simulations and visualizations of their results.
Authors can choose to share models under any of the Creative Commons licenses . Please submit an issue to request support for additional licenses.
